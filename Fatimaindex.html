I hold a Bachelor's degree in Special Education with a specialisation in Learning Disability. My work centres on building trusting, one-on-one relationships and small group connections with children aged 5 to 15 — because real learning begins when a child feels safe.

I support children across a wide spectrum of needs — Autism Spectrum Disorder, ADHD, Specific Learning Disabilities in reading, writing and maths, Cerebral Palsy, Down Syndrome, Sensory Processing Disorder, and Gifted learners. No two children are alike, and no two plans should be either.

Before setting a single goal, I take the time to truly understand each child — using diagnostic tools like DTLD, DTRD, GLAD, and the Aston Index alongside careful observation. Because the right support starts with the right understanding.

My Process

The IEP journey

A thoughtful, six-stage process that keeps the child — and the family — at the heart of every decision.

01 Referral & Screening

  • Identifying the performance gap
  • Screening with GLAD, Aston Index and checklists
  • Confirming the need for support

02 Baseline Assessment

  • Multi-stakeholder evaluation (SE, OT, SLP, Psych)
  • Establishing current level of performance
  • Parental and school concern alignment

03 Collaborative Programming

  • IEP meeting with full team
  • Aligning goals across parents, teachers and specialists
  • Formal placement in appropriate support category

04 SMART Goal Setting

  • Annual "big picture" targets
  • Short-term micro-step objectives
  • Specific, measurable, achievable, realistic and time-bound

05 Implementation

  • Accommodations — changing how they learn
  • Modifications — changing what they learn
  • Assistive technology integration

06 Monitoring & Review

  • Daily and weekly data tracking
  • Periodic progress reporting to stakeholders
  • Six-monthly review to reset or retire goals
